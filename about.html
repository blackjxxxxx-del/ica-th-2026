About the Conference

ICA Regional Hub Thailand 2026 brings together scholars, practitioners, and policymakers to explore communication inequalities and advance research collaboration across Thailand, the Mekong region, and the Global South.

What It Is

The ICA Regional Hub Thailand 2026 is a two-day academic conference held on 6–7 June 2026 at the Faculty of Communication Arts, Chulalongkorn University, Bangkok, Thailand.

It is an official concurrent event of the 76th Annual Conference of the International Communication Association (ICA), running in parallel with the main conference in Cape Town.

The Hub operates in a hybrid format, enabling in-person participation in Bangkok alongside live virtual attendance from anywhere in the world.

The conference is hosted by the Faculty of Communication Arts, Chulalongkorn University, and the Asian Network for Public Opinion Research (ANPOR), in partnership with additional co-hosting institutions to be confirmed.

Why Now

As a dynamic hub of Southeast Asia and the Mekong region, Thailand is confronting rapid digital transformation, expanding AI systems, platform-driven misinformation, and widening socio-economic divides that are reshaping communication in ways that often deepen — rather than resolve — inequality.

Yet the scholarly communities most deeply embedded in these realities — in Thailand and across the wider Global South — remain structurally underrepresented in flagship international journals and conferences.

At the moment when ICA 2026 centres on "Communication and Inequalities in Context," ICA-TH 2026 positions Thailand not merely as host, but as a convening centre for global dialogue.

From Bangkok, conversations expand outward — connecting Southeast Asia, the Global South, and the wider international community.

Who It Is For

The conference welcomes communication scholars at all career stages — from doctoral researchers to senior faculty — alongside media practitioners, platform policymakers, civil society advocates, journalists, and public health communicators working in or on the Thailand-Mekong-Asian region.

Both in-person attendance in Bangkok and virtual participation are fully supported.

Five Conference Objectives

Guiding the research agenda and shaping outcomes across both days of the conference.

01

Regional Research Capacity

Strengthen research capacity through mentorship, publication guidance, and methodological training for scholars from Thailand and the Mekong region.

02

Global Visibility

Amplify Thailand and Mekong scholarship in global ICA dialogue through hybrid programme integration and a live-streamed Global South Plenary.

03

Inclusive Knowledge Production

Challenge structural inequalities in academic publishing and promote contextually grounded theory from the Global South.

04

Regional Research Network

Establish the Thailand–Mekong Communication Research Consortium and develop thematic regional research working groups.

05

Academic & Policy Outputs

Produce the Bangkok Hub Report, special journal issue proposals, and collaborative multi-country research grants.
